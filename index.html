Code Proposal Architecture Evaluation

The framework first adapts an expressive IndexTTS2 model to Taiwanese with parameter-efficient tuning, then applies GRPO rewards to recover emotion, intensity, local emphasis, and intelligibility.

Abstract

We study the loss of emotional capability that occurs when an expressive text-to-speech model trained on a high-resource language is adapted to a low-resource language. Although the adapted model may stay intelligible, its emotional controllability, rhythm, emphasis, and prosodic richness can become flatter.

Our proposal builds on IndexTTS2 and treats Chinese-to-Taiwanese transfer as an emotion preservation problem. Instead of learning emotion from scratch, the method aims to retain the pretrained expressive prior while improving target-language pronunciation through lightweight adaptation and reinforcement learning based emotional realignment.

Method

1

Expressive Base Model

Start from a high-resource expressive IndexTTS2 model that already carries emotion and prosody priors.

2

Target-Language Adaptation

Use parameter-efficient modules such as adapters or LoRA to learn Taiwanese pronunciation while limiting catastrophic forgetting.

3

RL Emotional Realignment

Update the autoregressive text-to-semantic policy with rewards computed from decoded speech waveforms.

Composite Reward

R = λ1 Remo + λ2 Rint + λ3 Remp + λ4 RASR

Emotion classification, VAD-space intensity, pitch/energy emphasis, and ASR content rewards are balanced in one scalar signal.

GRPO with KL Anchor

maximize E[R log π(a|s)] - β KL(π || padapt)

The KL penalty keeps the policy close to the adapted reference model while exploring expressive improvements.

System Architecture

The policy observes input text, speaker timbre, duration constraints, and a target global emotion embedding. It generates discrete semantic speech tokens, which are decoded into audio and evaluated by reward models before GRPO updates the text-to-semantic transformer.

Evaluation Plan

The evaluation directly measures emotional forgetting and recovery across three stages: before adaptation, after naive or parameter-efficient adaptation, and after RL optimization. The protocol combines objective speech metrics with human listening tests for emotional intensity and local emphasis.

Objective quality WER / SS / ES

ASR word error rate, speaker similarity, and emotion similarity or accuracy track intelligibility, identity, and target emotion.

Emotion retention ERG

Emotion Retention Gap quantifies degradation after adaptation; recovery ratio measures how much RL restores.

Human evaluation MOS / EIT / EAT

Listeners rate quality, similarity, prosody, and emotion while comparing intensity and perceived emphasis positions.

Preliminary Baseline

As a pilot Taiwanese TTS baseline, 12 SARC laboratory members rated CosyVoice2 on 20 intelligibility and naturalness sentences plus 10 voice-similarity sentences, producing 600 subjective rating samples.

4.16 Similarity MOS
4.41 Intelligibility MOS
4.37 Naturalness MOS
4.31 Mean MOS

The high baseline quality motivates the next step: moving beyond intelligibility and naturalness to test whether emotional expressiveness can be preserved and recovered during low-resource adaptation.
